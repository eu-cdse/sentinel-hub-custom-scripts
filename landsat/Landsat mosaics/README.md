# Landsat Bi-monthly Mosaics

The [Landsat Bi-monthly Mosaics](https://documentation.dataspace.copernicus.eu/Data/ComplementaryData/Landsat_Bi_monthly_Mosaics.html) dataset represents a global, cloud-free, and reconstructed historical Landsat spectral bands along with a clear sky mask at a spatial resolution of 30 meters and temporal resolution of bimonthly intervals from 1997 onwards. The dataset was generated by OpenGeoHub using the GLAD Landsat ARD version 2 as the primary input for temporal aggregation and the imputation of missing values. As a result the product delivers complete, gap-filled time series of surface reflectance. This is one of the major outputs of the Open-Earth-Monitor Cyberinfrastructure (OEMC) project. The OEMC project has received funding from the European Union’s Horizon Europe research and innovation programme under grant agreement No. 101059548.

The dataset reduces the original 1.4PB GLAD Landsat ARD collection to approximately 100TB by removing contaminated part of images, performing weighted temporal aggregation, and applying SWA (Seasonally Weighted Average) for reconstructing the times-series associated with each pixel. All data are stored as 8-bit Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFFs (COG) with deflate compression applied to chunks of 1,024 × 1,024 pixels.
